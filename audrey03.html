Three Months

    Cooing and Lifting Head
    Narrated by Ellis and Elvin · May 2017
    udrey is lots of fun these days. She loves to play and cannot stop smiling and laughing. When she does not get her milk on time, she cries at a ear-piercing decibel. Her weight gain accelerated with the volume of milk she is gulping down.

    2 May, Tuesday (Week 14) - Vaccination 2

    Head Circumference: 39.5 cm. Body Length: 59 cm. Weight: 6 Kg.
    Well, I am counting down to the end of my maternity leave. A part of me cannot bear to leave Audrey for 8 long hours everyday once I started work. And yet, there is another part of me who is looking forward to a new lifestyle.
    As usual, I never stop snapping photos of Audrey’s every movement. Look at that, Audrey ‘Phelps’ in the making. That expression totally nails it. Roarrrr‼
    Taking a break off the stressful renovation work underway, we brought Audrey for her vaccination. Okay, that was another mind boggling event. The dilemma is always there. Vaccination proves to be better than without. However, it hurts seeing my previous one getting poke, not once but twice each time. Ouch ouch! My darling has been really brave. Her cries are just at that moment and she calms down quickly. Her second Hep B jab in March was impressive! Today, she cried a little more but I was still proud of her endurance to manage it. Thumbs up, Audrey! I hope you treat your every challenges in life this way. Perseverance.
    Audrey cannot walk yet so she needs to get your attention when she is tired of lying down. The world is boring looking up at legs and ceiling. She wants to be carried around and smell the fresh air. Carrying her while sitting on the sofa does not count as carrying. She will whine when you do that. I suspect she has a built-in butt-sitting detector installed somewhere. We are left with no choice but to carry her and walk arond. Carrying a 6 Kg baby for an extended period is a test on your arm muscles. I should get nicely toned muscles by the end of this ordeal.

    7 May, Sunday (Week 14) - 100 Days

    Head Circumference: 39.5 cm. Body Length: 59 cm.
    A hundred days is a big milestone for a baby to reach in the past. In keeping up with the Teochew tradition, we are celebrating Audrey’s hundredth days with lots of fanfare. Relatives are invited and food is prepared. There are rituals to perform. There is a lucky bath for Audrey with pomelo leaves to bless her with good health. Now, it is time to dress up like a princess and smile at everybody.
    This cute princess loves to be carried so everyone takes turn. Here is Xiaoyi, my dad and Dayi. According to the Princess protocol, one must throw a little tantrum every so often to qualify as a princess. That way, the people around you will be at your beck and call. A princess needs to have many headbands, peferably changing to new one every hour to keep the look fresh.
    The main event of the day is the feast. At the feast, we have Marie biscuits, persimons, apples, mandarin oranges, prawns, chicken drumstick, tou fu and seaweed. Audrey gets a whiff of the food items in sequence. Each food item has a symbolic meaning that Ellis knows.
    After the feast, it’s time to put on the jewelleries. There are gold necklace, bracelet and anklet from the grandmothers and great-grandmother. There are coins with Rooster drawings that we bought for her. This symbolises wealth. We will like Audrey to have abundance of wealth in her heart and mind. Finally, as the 100th day draws to a close, we created a mini placard with coloured markers and a big envelope. Then we took a family photo to remember this day.

    14 May, Sunday (Week 15) - Mother's Day

    Head Circumference: 39.5 cm. Body Length: 59.5 cm.
    Can you believe it? It’s Mother’s Day this week. We have a new mother this time — Ellis! She sure is excited about being a mother. Two mothers means two times the cake — a dark chocolate and a white chocolate. Audrey is up for the occasion too, smiling and shouting for the photos. She must be looking forward to becoming a mother some day to pass forward the love she is receiving.

    17 May, Wednesday (Week 16) - Staycation

    Head Circumference: 40 cm. Body Length: 62 cm.
    Little Audrey is growing up fast. Look at her many expressions and she begins to relate and play with you! At the same time, look at these “bah bah” cheeks and thighs! Definitely growing, Audrey!
    We were in the midst of managing the ever-messy renovation of our new house when Xiaoyi kindly suggested to bring Audrey for a staycation. My first reaction was really negative. I could not bear to think of Audrey staying away for me, even though it was only a night. LOL. Separation anxiety began with me. After several discussions, we realised that this arrangement would be the best as we would be able to fully concentrate on the renovation while Xiaoyi and my MIL could enjoy the staycation with Audrey. Hence, we agreed.
    It felt weird for the first night. I could sleep well as I would habitually wake up to check… on the empty cot. Well, not entirely as I would still need to express the milk in the night. The next morning, I left the house with Elvin and made my way to Sentosa. Oh I miss Audrey. But judging from the photos Xiaoyi sent the night before, Audrey was coping well...without me! When I got there, we went for breakfast at Toast Box and then walked around USS area, not forgetting the many photo sessions for Audrey! Audrey also tried, for the first time in her life, to touch the swimming pool. We filmed it and saw that she was actually keeping her legs in. It might have felt weird for her.
    Elvin joined us after his work and we had Imperial Treasure for dinner. Yummy as always! We went back to the room and settled down before turning in to bed. It was a huge family suite. There was a king sized bed, a double sofa bed and a loft bed! It could comfortably sleep 6 people! As Elvin needed to work the next day, we woke up really early to get some family photos. The morning sunlight was providing a nice lighting for our photos. Although it was a wefie, we thought the photos turned out well! YAY! Family photo, finally! I could not wait to share on my instagram.
    We went to Candylicious for a walk. The products there were so cute, perfect for photo taking. We cushioned the mini trolley and placed Audrey in it. The trolley fitted her well! And Mummy put on funny hats and took photos with her. Haha, luckily we were not spotted by the staff there!

    19 May, Friday (Week 16) - Family Bonding Week

    Head Circumference: 40 cm. Body Length: 62 cm.

    21 May, Sunday (Week 16) - Who does Audrey resembles more?

    The eternal question that friends and families ask when they look at the baby, “Does baby look like Mommy and Daddy?” To answer this, Ellis has painstakingly dug up old photos that is at the same angle, resize them and did a side-by-side collage. The photo resolution has improved drastically in the last thirty years when you compare it with the old photos. Based on my careful analysis, Audrey has taken genes from each of us. She has my eyes — the end of the eye tapers off the ends, that thick band of eyebrows and the long eyelashes. She has Ellis’ mouth — the deep caves extending from the side of the nose to the mouth and that v-shape jaw line. Audrey’s nose is unique though. I thought Ellis and my nose look the same. As Audrey’s facial feature is changing gradually, we will do another round of “Who does Audrey resembles more” battle when she is one year old.

    24 May, Wednesday (Week 17) - Lunch with Dylan

    It was the second time I brought Audrey out, alone. It was kind of a milestone for me, feeling independent and fulfilling as a mother. I felt the importance that someone has to depend on me. I felt important and there was this strong sense of responsibility. I felt proud to show people, my daughter. Hehe. We met at Ichiban Boshi at Parkway. I took a cab over and Audrey was really calm during the ride. I prepared toys and milk just in case she became cranky but thank goodness, she was cooperative and enjoyed her every moment out! Dora and I shared alot of funny moments and tiring times of being first time mummies. I was glad that we had each other to share our stories. It is like “Finally, someone understands me!”

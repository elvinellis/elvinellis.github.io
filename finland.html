    Honeymoon in Helsinki
    Narrated by Ellis · January 2016
    metropolitan city filled with Art Nouveau, Neo-classicism, and Functionalism architectures. It is no wonder that Helsinki hails for its design and high technology. Look at that pretty street on a random day! We got up early before the sun rose. The color of the sky and the lit buildings exude such romance. I could fall in love here forever.
    Glo Hotel Kluuvi was our accommodation for the next four nights, a ten-minutes walk from Helsinki Railway Station. It is located at the heart of the shopping paradise and many places of interests. Furthermore, it is a short walk to Esplanadi Park, Kauppatori Ferry Terminal, and the Old Market Hall. An excellent location for tourists! Not forgetting that the spacious and comfortable rooms, and the sumptuous breakfast buffet every morning.

    Esplanadi Park & Kappeli Restaurant

    Esplandi park was still lit with Christmas decorations. I exhaled, “How can a park be so beautiful!” There were fallen snows on the pathways. Due to the small increase in temperature, the snow became icy. We threaded lightly while protecting our cameras. Nothing must happen to our cameras even if we fall!
    We passed a posh-looking restaurant at the park. When the night fell and the lights turned on, the restaurant looks so elegant and romantic. I think it will be a superb place for wedding proposal! We didn’t have the chance to try the food when we were there but I would definitely hope that Elvin will surprise me some years down the road when we are back to Helsinki. Elvin, the restaurant is Kappeli!

    Sunrise and Islands

    We woke up early in the morning and got ourselves ready to catch the sunrise. We had passed this area once when we were on the city tour bus yesteray. We thought that it might be a good vantage point for a nice scenery shot. Just down the Old Market Hall was the Olympia Terminal. After more walking on the icy pavements, we finally reached. A serene area of high-end residentials and embassies overseeing the rather frozen Baltic Sea and many small islands. Along that Ehrenstromintie Road, we saw Soumenlinna from afar too. Just before the shore, the waves had frozen in time.
    As the sun rose, the sky displayed a spectrum of colours. It felt like sunset at times. We stopped for a moment to enjoy this magnificent sky while holding each other’s hand. Romance is simple.
    Occasionally, we had locals on their morning jog with such a great backdrop. I’m envious! We didn’t want to stop snapping photos along this bank but our stomach wanted to. After seeing the sun rises to the tip of Soumenlinna Tower, we bid goodbye to this wonderful location.

    Old Market Hall

    A distinct building seen as we walked out to the Esplanadi Park. It was so bustling on Saturday when we got there. Vibrant colours seen along each aisle from the vast variety of vegetables, fruits, salmon and more salmon! The stallholders were very friendly to us — their potential customers.
    We had salmon soup, sandwiches and beer. We also chanced upon the dark rye thins we tried in the Hotel Buffet Breakfast! No second thoughts for fans of dark rye bread and sourdough! Of course, we did not forget about souvenirs for our family and friends. What a splendid way to end the day in Helsinki.

    Ekberg Cafe

    On our one-day city tour in Helsinki, we were introduced of Ekberg Cafe in Bulevardi Street. It is a well-known cafe for the locals and tourists. Hence, during one of our free time in the evening, we made our way there from the hotel by foot.
    Seemingly near… it was quite a long walk, we finally stepped into the cafe. We were welcomed by an array of beautiful cakes and pastries. The ambience is cosy. It wasn’t very crowded and there were mostly locals having their afternoon tea there.
    We sat down and a friendly staff came over to recommend a few dishes for us. We ordered a salmon soup and venison pasta for our mains. A cup of coffee and hot chocolate with a couple of cakes to round off the meal.
    I must say, the salmon soup is superb. I never knew a salmon soup can be made so deliciously! The pasta is good too. Venison tasted like duck meat to me.
    After a satisfying meal, we popped over to its shop beside it and bought a jar of Lingon Berries Jam. It didn’t have the synthetic sugary taste like other jams we see in the market. I love the tinch of sourness in the sweet jam. I think Lingon Berries Jam tasted better than any other jams I had in my life.

    Soumenlinna — the Fortress of Finland

    We have gotten the Helsinki Card which allow us to get free city tours, entry to museums and ferries. We went for a 2-hour city tour around Helsinki before taking a ferry by HSL from Kauppatori Terminal to Soumenlinna — The Sea Fortress. It was a weird feeling sailing through the frozen sea and a rather bumpy ride too!
    It was not only Helsinki that we explored. We took bus in the day to visit a nearby town called Porvoo. When we returned on a different bus in the evening, we got worried. We were not familiar with the roads and the sky was getting dark. It gets dark early during the winter. We kept seeing Helsingfors on the highway sign and thought we were headed for another town. Finally, I asked a local on the bus if we were on the right bus and yes we were. It turned out that Helsingfors is the Swedish name for Helsinki. After five days in Helsinki, we flew out to Nellim for the second part of our exciting adventure.

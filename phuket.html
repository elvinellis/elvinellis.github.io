Phuket

Southern island set in the sea

Narrated by Elvin
July 2014
Tucked away in the southern corner of Thailand is the small island of Phuket. It has clean sea and beautiful landscape. Our getaway to Phuket was a short flight. We began our journey with the exploration of the islands. Sitting on the upper deck of our boat, we sped towards the islands.

Canoeing

The best way to get close to the rocks of the islands is by a canoe. The boat moored and canoes on the lower deck were unloaded. Each couple was assigned a guide to paddle the canoe. The guides were highly skilled in manouvering around the nooks and crannies. Our role was to sit back, relax and enjoy the view.
Going through a cave under the rock, we got to the other side where there is a small clearing.
Our canoe reached another cave. This time the opening allowed only one to pass each time. We had to queue and jostle our turn. Getting closer to the cave, it dawned on us how tiny the entrance was. To get through the cave, we had to lie flat on the canoe. Gathering momentum, our canoe slipped through the hole as all three of us lay flat. The ceiling of the cave rushed past and darkness engulfed us momentarily. Inside the cave, the ceiling was slightly higher but we were not cleared yet. The guide navigated the boat by pushing against the rocks on the side. This trip is not one for the claustophobic and faint-hearted.

Swimming

What is the best thing about a double-decked boat? Jumping off into the sea for a swim. There is an adrenaline rush from the fall and then the cold water hits me. There's nothing like cooling off in cold water on a hot day.

James Bond Island

On the Kho Phing Kan island, there is a famous limestone tower karst sticking out in the sea called Ko Tapu. It is the result of years of sea water erosion. It is also called James Bond Island because some scene of the movies were shot here.
As a popular destination, there is a huge amount of tourists flocking here and we added ourselves to that crowd. Docking our boat on near the shore is a careful process of sliding in between the countless others.

Island Safari

We are here are the Island Safari today to look at animals. The highlight of the place is the elephant ride. Elephants are massive creatures. They are wobbly to ride on as they lumbered around. The hairs on them are thick like bristles. There are mangosteen trees in the forest and our elephant trainer climbed up the trees to pluck some for us. The elephant got a treat too.
Although it was a fun experience to ride on one, that would be the last time. After the ride, I witnessed a sad scene where a young elephant was chained to be domesticated for riding. I later read that elephants are difficult to breed and any captive elephants are captured from the wild.

Patong Beach

Patong beach is a long and flat beach in front of our hotel. The waves makes a dramatic entrance, piling up on each other and roaring as many of them break on the shore. The water is so clean and clear, it could be mistaken for spring water.
The sun set in front of Patong beach. Everyone love a sunset. Strangers stand, sit and hang around the beach for the spectacular view. When the evening comes, dinner is just right right around the corner. Hawker's food and restaurants lined the roads that snaked in behind the beach.

Ban Thai

We put up at Ban Thai - a lovely resort at Patong beach. There is a nice balcony and louvered windows to play with. The resort is spacious and relatively sparse during our stay there. That means peace and quiet.

Departure

Phuket was awesome with all the island, clean water and beautiful weather. We toasted to that with warm food and cold beer on the flight back home.

Jeju Island

Province Island

Narrated by Elvin
April 2013
eju island (Jejudo) is the largest island off the coast of the Korean Peninsula, and the main island of Jeju Province of South Korea. The island lies in the Korea Strait, southwest of South Jeolla Province. We arrived here on a frigid night with cold winds blasting into our faces. After a quick dinner, we rested at the hotel.
The next day, we went to the Jeju Folk Village. On our way in, we were greeted by beautiful flowers and gardens. Spring is in the air! Ellis happily hugs a phallic stone statue that is said to bless women with babies.
This is a massive place that was recreated to depict the olden days of Jeju. In this folk village, you get to see the old houses made of mud, wood and leaves. Models of villagers show a snapshot of their daily life. The domesticated animals like ducks, cows, and pigs are real, however. Freshwater is hard to come by on a small island so the inhabitants resort to collecting rainwater in vats. By tying up dried grass and creating a platform to direct water into the vat, it creates a larger surface area to collect the rain.
Ellis and I tried our hands on the household chores like a married couple. I used wooden rollers to straighten clothes by beating them. The iron was not invented then. Ellis ground oats in a huge mortar to make me a delicious meal. Oh boy, I'm such a lucky guy.

ATV Joyride

We are going on a ride on an ATV (All-terrain Vehicle) in our awesome helmets. We look cute in the photo. Ellis sat behind me while I powered the ATV. This is one powerful monster, thundering through the plains and charging up the hill with ease. Our hearts sank a little when it rolled down the hills. We had so much fun and it was over too soon.

Trick Art Museum

As the name suggests, this museum presents art pieces designed to trick your eyes. For example, this portrait of a lady flaunting her posterior, you will see that her legs look short and fat or long and slim depending on where you are standing. Like many things in life, it's all about the perspective.
Ther were interesting stuff in the souvenir shop: hats! We tried them all. Ellis looks radiantly happy.

Teddy Bear Museum

We found where all the teddy bears have been hiding. They lead a secret life in this museum. Each section showcases a culture or an event. My favorite is the teddy bears attending a traditional Korean wedding ceremony. The groom and bride bow to each other under the tent.
Outside the museum, there are teddy bear statues too. This cool-looking bear deserves a punch as he just stole my love.

Yonduam Dragon Head Rock

Jeju has plenty of volcanic rocks and they are what fills this shore. Amongst all the rocks, one stood out. One that is mysteriously shaped like the head of a dragon. From this angle, you can see it fiercely breathing fire out of its mouth. There is slight round bulge that is the eye and at the back of its head, antlers protrude.

Korean Cuisine

In this last part, we have all the Korean meals we ate on the trip. The dishes are a vibrant display of colors - red, white, green, yellow, brown, black, and white. They tend to come in many small dishes spread across the table. I especially like the kimchi, seaweed, and fat bean sprouts. Good chopstick skill is a must for an enjoyable eating experience. Another style of eating is the hotpot soup filled to the brim with vegetables, mushroom, and meat that is popular with Asians. Breakfast tends to be congee and hot buns.
We left Jeju Island back to Korea after a short two days. We will remember the scenic life, gusty cold wind and the laughters we left behind.

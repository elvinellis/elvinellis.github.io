Third Trimester

    A kicking babe
    Narrated by Ellis and Elvin · November 2016
    hird trimester is a guessing game. If the estimated due date is the bullseye of a dart board, then the actual delivery date is anywhere on the board. As early as thirty-seven weeks or as late as forty-one weeks. As if that variance is not nerve-wrecking enough, we have to second-guess fake contractions and doubting if Mommy just peed or has the water bag burst. Amidst the waiting, we are palpably excited as first-timers. We can hardly hold ourselves back from buying “omg-it’s-so-cute-I-wanna-die” clothes. As for Audrey, she is busy growing fat, kicking her legs, pushing her tiny fists against womb and having hiccups.

    30 November, Wednesday (Week 30) - Doctor's Talk at SCGS

    Mommy's waist: 37.5", Mommy's weight: 65.3 kg
    SCGS 7.30pm.
    We are back at SCGS for a second time and the seats at their hall still kills my back. These seats are made for a younger and smaller body. At least the content of the talk is interesting. The head of CordLife laboratory is giving a presentation on the science behind cord blood and cord lining banking. He is a total geek through and through. After thirty minutes of scientific jargons, technical procedures, miracle stories, he ended his talk with a video and left the stage. Woa. The marketing department sent him here to “educate” the public and he took it literally. What a guy. But I’m sold on this cord banking technology based on its life-changing properties. It sounds like the elixir of life and I am betting my money on it. For science!

Mummy, on the other hand, was busy picking the beansprout husks. It was really time consuming and energy draining but it was satisfying to complete bowls of husks.     1 January, Sunday (Week 35) - Rock-a-bye Audrey

Keep up the good work and continue to absorb as much nutrients as possible! Mummy is getting more tired with each passing day… But I am looking forward to your arrival!

Sometimes I can feel that small little foot of hers sticking out from my belly. Some other times I can feel her punch so close to my groin that I though she is already coming out. Suddenly, the pregnancy journey is ending soon. Time flies. I am feeling excited for Audrey’s arrival and also nervous about the labour. I can’t seem to calm down now too.     16 January, Monday (Week 37) - Pillow Case

    • GA: 37 Weeks. EDD: 08-02-2017
    • Heart Rate 140 BPM. Mommy's Weight: 66.8 kg
    Mommy wants a pillow case for Audrey’s husk pillow so here I am making one. First, we drew out the design on paper and then measured the pillow’s dimension. Next, Mommy measured 104cm x 22cm on the fabric. I double-checked the measurement and cut it out. As they say, measure twice, cut once. When the fabric is cut, the edges will unravel so I sew a hem. To get to the final shape, I folded the fabric in half with a flap at the opening. The hardest of sewing is the spatial visualisation of how to sew the fabric together inside out. After both edges were sewn down, I flipped the fabric the right side out and tada, we have a pillow case. After a quick steam iron to flatten the seams and corners, we put the pillow case on and congratulate ourselves on the nice work.
    I took photos for Mommy in her pretty pink dress. She is heavily pregnant now and this is probably her last photoshoot before her tummy becomes flat again. She looks so pretty in this dress.

It all happened so fast. She started crying as the nurse cleaned her and suctioned the remaining fluid in her nose. At the weighing station, Audrey tipped the scale at 2.69 kg.
    Here is Audrey. Staring at me with wide big eyes, curious with the world and what is happening. I sang her a lullaby that we always did when she was inside the womb. A happy feeling swept over me as I watched her kicking in the warmer. Mommy is exhausted on the bed so she can’t get up to see Audrey. I was busy taking photos and videos, then ferrying it over for Mommy to see how cute our baby is.

    The Backstory of the White-throated Kingfisher and the Shimmering Silver Egg

    Before Ellis was pregnant, I had a perculiar dream. It came to me one night during the family trip to Penang. I usually do not remember my dreams when I awake. Yet, this dream burned vividly into my memory. It was perculiar because the dream felt like a message. In that dream, I found myself in a kampung village. I was the only inhabitant because I did not recall seeing another face. In the next scene, I was peering into a nest that had two eggs in it. The eggs were special. They were a size between a quail and a chicken egg. One egg was a glittering gold translucent and the other egg was a shimmering silvery translucent. Both were emitting a calm light from within. On the edge of the nest was a white-throated kingfisher. The bird was not afraid and watched me intently. I wanted the golden egg and snatched it. But the kingfisher stopped me with its beak. It then surprised me by picking up the silver egg and putting it into my palm. It looked at me as if to say this was a gift and then flew away in flash of red and blue. I looked at the silver egg with curiosity as it shimmered in my palm.
    I woke up from the dream puzzled. The next month Ellis was pregnant. When I recounted my dream to Ellis later, both of us thought that the silver egg was probably a baby.
    Nine months later, we were on the way to Thomson Medical for the delivery of Audrey and I saw a lone white-throated kingfisher from the car. It was perching on a tree at the Tampines flyover. Although, this sounds far-fetched but I thought the white-throated kingfisher was the one from my dream. And it was there that day to send off the shimmering silver egg that is about to hatch.

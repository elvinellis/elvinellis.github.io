    Pulau Ubin
    1 May

    From the pier at Changi, the bumboat takes about ten minutes to get to Ubin island. Ellis and I were going there to take photos of birds. Along the way, fine spray of salt water and noxious engine fumes hit us. Once on the shore, we trekked around the island, armed with our cameras. We got to a remote end when we started hearing rustlings in the trees. The sounds followed us as we walked down the mud path. Clearly, we were not alone. I spotted monkeys in the canopy. When I look at one in the eyes, he gave a call that was mixture of laugh and scream which appears to be taunting me when combined. As the monkeys outnumbered us, we decide to back-tracked and head on to the western part of the island.
    There was an abandoned granite quarry that is filled with water. It was here we saw eagles play-fighting in the air. As an eagle swooped down on another from the top, the bottom one flips out to reveal its talons. Both of them momentarily locked talons before breaking away. I wasn’t sure if it was courtship or fighting. We also saw oriental pied hornbills, magpie robins and a red butterfly that I can’t identify. I took photos of the local flora and fauna — a olive-backed bulbul and a female metallic-winged sunbird.
    Soon, the mid-day sun was beating down mercilessly at us and we retreated back to Singapore. At Changi Village, we took a cool respite at Chock Full of Beans. This cafe is famous for it’s coffee foam art so we ordered iced coffees. Mine was Hello Kitty and Ellis got a bear. We reluctantly crushed these beautiful art and sipped on our drinks.

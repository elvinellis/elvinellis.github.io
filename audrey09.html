    Babbling
    Narrated by Ellis and Elvin · October 2017
    fter eight months, this little girl babbles. While she was mastering her crawl, she stayed silent most of the time. That got us worried. These days, she practises her screams and shouts to get our attention and get the things she wants. She learned a cool trick on her own — snapping her finger with a “tuck” sound. Not bad at all. She is unafraid of stranger and new places. Getting cozy with her surroundings in minutes. The only thing that scares her is the xiaomi robot vacuum. But I suspect that won’t be for long.

    28 October, Saturday - Generations

    I have been thinking about generations. How do we define one human generation from the next. There is no obvious line that separates one from the next. So I am thinking generations are like fish scales. Why fish scales? Because I have been descaling salmon skin and noticed how one scale overlaps the other in a never-ending pattern. Each fish scale would be a generation. As we grow older in time, each generation moves forward on its own. Six years would be a nice period to differentiate one generation from the next. If someone is less than six years older than me, I consider him to be in the same generation.
    That makes Audrey five and a half fish scales away from me. When she grows up, things will be so different. When I was young, being a software engineer was not even a career. When Audrey grows up, programming will be a common skill. Working in space will become a norm. Maybe she will even start a family on Mars. To think that all these will happen in five and a half fish scales.

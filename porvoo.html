Medieval Town

Narrated by Ellis
January 2016

Porvoo - The Old Medieval Town

We took a coach out of Helsinki city to Porvoo, one of the six Medieval towns of Finland, just an hour away from city centre. It was as if we had time travelled. They have the famous red shore house that created a unique backdrop for the Porvoo river - Porvoonjoki. We notice that on the other side of the riverbank are slowly filling up with newer and modern looking houses. It is quite a stark constrast from this side of the old town.
Porvoo is famous also because it is the home of Johan Ludwig Runeburg - Finland National Poet. They even have a tart named after him - Runeburg Tart. We went to Cabriole Restaurant that served buffet for our lunch. We had the sweet Runeburg Tart there as well. The texture of the tart is similar to Financier with strawberry jam in it. Elvin also surprised me with their famous macarons although I know he doesn't like sweet stuff - That's sweet.
We then took a walk along their heritage trail with the map we had gotten from the tourist's centre. Walking along the trail feels like walking in a fairy tale scene. We passed by their Town Hall and Porvoo Cathedral. Every house along the street is about to 3 storeys with hip roofs and they are donned in different pastel colours. The blue house is beside the yellow and then pink. How sweet.

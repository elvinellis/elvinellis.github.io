I got down on one knee and ask her the question in my bravest voice, “Will you marry me?” She started crying tears of joy and agreed. I took her hand to put on the ring but she pulled it away. It turned out that I took the wrong hand. She presented her left hand and I put the ring on her finger.
    In celebration of this wonderful moment, we went to Orchard for a singing session. Walking past a store, we bought miniature Cony and Brown from the current Line sticker frenzy. They represent us.
    In the evening, we took a romantic stroll at Fort Canning. I had made reservations at the Gattopardo — an Italian Grill and Pizza Bar — and we walked in the restaurant for dinner. We sat at our cozy table and soaked in the sweet nectar of love. Ellis was intoxicated so she took blurry photos of me — also known as lomography. This was the only useable photo I had of myself at this posh restaurant that day. Anyway, the food was great. We had truffle fettuccine and some exotic fish, that I can’t remember, grilled in an exotic combination of herbs, spice and vegetables. We finished off on a sweet note with a tiramisu with a candle on it and the words “Happy Anniversary” beautifully written on the plate.

    Diamond and Ring

    The proposal ring was custom-made from Jann Paul. After describing an initial design that we had in mind, they came back with CAD rendition on how the rings would like. The prongs that holds up the diamond was a heart-shaped cut-out at the bottom. It looks cute at the beginning but will be a tad childish for Ellis thirty years from now.
    So we went for a design that can withstand the test of time. The prongs rises from the ring like a twisting branches and latches onto the diamond. The diamond ebellishments on the edge of the ring provides the finishing touch.

    Lemon Meringue Tart
    31 August

    Meringues are weird stuff. When I read the recipe on to make them, terms like soft peak and stiff peak confuses the hell out of me. When I finally succeed in making them, it still confuses the hell out of me how I succeeded. Well, this is how it is made. You start with egg whites, after carefully removing the yolk, and start whipping the transparent whites. After five minutes of whipping, all you get are plenty of bubbles on the surface. After ten minutes, you get more tiny bubbles. The recipe doesn’t say how long to whip it, it only says you whip till the egg whites has a solid enough structure to form a peak. Now I am not sure if my eggs are rotten or if I wasn’t whipping fast enough. The thing about meringue is you need patience and an arm that is skilled at making repeated motion quickly. Once in a while, I rest my aching biceps. Soon, I managed to get a soft peak. Adding sugar and whipping further, the egg whites finally turned glossy pearl white. It’s unbeliveable. How does transparent egg whites become glossy pearl white just by hitting it repeatedly? And what must be going through the mind of the person who invented meringue?
    Anyway, time to make lemon meringue tart. We had already prepare the lemon filling and the base. We only need to top it with the meringue. I tried styling my tart then decided it was too much trouble and dumped the whole lot it. Anyway, it turned out toastly brown and yummy. Now I can take on the world as a desert chef.

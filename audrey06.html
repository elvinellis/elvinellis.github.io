    15 August, Tuesday - Story Telling

    Audrey is lazing around and staring into space after waking up. I started her routine morning story-telling. I wasn’t sure if she is paying attention as she seems to get a kick out of kicking her leg at me. Eventually, I had enough. I grabbed her and sat her on my lap. She is happy either way with the attention she is getting. One day she will understand the words from the book but not today.

    17 August, Thursday - Hep B Vaccination

    Audrey has a high pain threshold from my observations. This morning, we took her for a Hep. B vaccine. She is conscious of what is happening now so I was expecting her to have a screaming fit as the long needle pierce her skin followed by the uncomfortable rush of fluid into the body. She cried out loud for thirty seconds. That’s all it took for her to calm down. Apart from this incident, there were also several times she bumped her head and she recovered from crying within a minute and she will laugh if you amused her. Pain means little to this girl.
    Both of us are half-naked here. It’s time for Audrey to cool off in the bath on this day. She is unusually jovial to be without clothes. Maybe it is the free and wild feeling you get when naked. Maybe Audrey is just being Audrey. Always smiling and happy.

    19 August, Saturday - Crouching Audrey, Hidden Canvas

    I am upset. I ordered a canvas print and the postman was supposed to have left it at the door. It was missing since last night and I cannot contact the postman. There is nothing I can do at the moment so I watched Audrey. She repeatedly raises her butt, crouches and then prones back down. She doesn’t know she can crawl. She is experimenting with her legs and testing its limits. When she is tired of her morning exercise, she demands to be carried. Suddenly, the door bell rings.
    It is the postman at the door delivering a package. I ask him if he delivered a package to my house yesterday afternoon. By a stroke of luck, it was him indeed. Now, where did he put my canvas print? The postman was bemused and opened the door to service meter room. Lo and behold, my package was there. I swear it was locked when I tried to force open last night. After he left, I tried to open it and I could not. Ellis couldn’t either. We never figured out to this day how the postman opened the locked door.
    Putting that aside, I can’t wait to unbox my print. After slicing through the box, I got my canvas print. It was awesome. This photo was taken when we went for our honeymoon at Finland. It was a river that never freeze even in the thick of the winter. We drank from the river and the water was pure, cold and sweet. The snow was very thick and covered everything except the river. If you look close enough, you can see the snow flakes falling in the foreground. There is shiny gold yellow object in the river bed which I decided it was gold. Ellis possibly rolled her eyes when she first heard my proposition.
    Audrey is busy practising her crouching skill. I think she is amused by the challenge of balancing on four limbs straight. Her curiosity drives her to keep trying. She is better at this compared to this morning. The speed at which she learn is swift.

South Korea

First trip together

Narrated by Elvin
April 2013

Seoul

This was our first trip overseas and my first time in South Korea and Jeju. We started in Dohwa-dong, Seoul where we put up at Best Western Hotel for the night. We hit the streets of Myeong-dong for some shopping fun. It was crowded with some jostling here and there. It reminded me of Bugis street with its weekend crowd. Push-carts along the roads sold women accessories, caps, scarves, gloves, bags, and shoes. Lots of shops sold facial products which South Korea is famous for.
We passed many food stalls along the way. Feeling hungry, we ducked into a Dak Galbi store. Dak Galbi is a one-dish affair. Marinated chicken or pork is dumped onto a hot plate along with tteok (rice cake) and vegetables. You stir-fry the sizzling meat till it is hot and then praise yourself for being a fantastic cook. The big metal barrier protected us from the sputtering sauce and gave us small-head syndrome in photos. After dinner, we took a walk in the cool weather and basked in the lighted displays of the shopping malls.

Everland

We had a wild time at the largest theme park in South Korea. Large spacious paths lined with flowers led visitors to different sections. We bought candy floss, candies and cute head bands. Being in a theme park like this makes an adult like me feel like a child again. There were plenty of rides like roller coasters, water rides, laser tag, train rides and merry go round.
After the rides, we went to view the animals at the Zoo-topia world. Having a zoo inside in a theme park makes it two times the fun. The highlight was the Liger, the child of a male lion and a female tiger. This perculiar sight rarely happens which explains its popularity and long queue to see it. Sitting in comfort of the bus, you got a close view of its features. It had the head and body shape of a lion but it has no mane and has a faded stripes spots on its body. There is always a flood of sadness in me when looking at caged animals. Such a majestic creature should be out there in the wild. As our bus rolled along the path, bears approached it and lumbered away when it realised food is sealed behind glass and steel.
The other small critters like the fox, beavers and jackass penguins were equally fun to watch. They kept themselves busy in their own world - chasing each other around and fighting for food.

Traditional Wedding

Korean traditional wedding is a colorful affair. The couples wore bright colors of red, blue, yellow, green and white. Following the Korean custom, the couples will bow, kneel, kowtow, exchange wine in the prescence of your parents, families and friends. They then live happily ever after.
At a Korean culture workshop, Ellis and I got to play the roles of the bride and groom. We wore the costumes and went through the ceremony while a teacher explains the significance of each step and action.

Cherry Blossom

Cherry blossom was the highlight of the trip. The abnormal weather in the recent decade meant the start of spring was more unpredictable than the past. We were lucky to be here at the time where the cherry trees felt it was a comfortable temperature to bloom. Once the bloom started, an unstoppable waves of white and pink flowers washed over the forest, parks and road side. The trees do not have any fresh leaves during the bloom. Man have been entranced by cherry blossoms since time immemorial. The display calms you and charms you. There is an undescribable beauty and universal appeal. The branches ripples and the flowers sways in breeze. The flowers fall to the ground to form a colorful carpet that welcome you to enjoy this fleeting moment.
The moment is short indeed. The bloom lasts fourteen days. Soon, the flowers will all be gone battered by the wind and rain. The fallen flowers on the roads meant that this period is also the busiest for the road cleaner in the year too. We also played in the field of canola flowers. The numerous plants grew to waist level and spread out neverending into the horizon. In the background, there were cherry blossoms and old houses. It feels like a dream to stand in the field of gold.

Traditional Village

Not far awhile, there was a traditional village. An area that has withstood the test of time. Old buildings were preserved and restored to retain its original design. Pillars and door frames were carved out of thick tree trunks. The low wall were made of stone plastered together with mud and capped with tiles that had elaborate symbols. The roads and surroundings was clean. Koreans have a penchant of keeping things neat and tidy. Their culture promotes love for their environment and nature.
In a courtyard stands several vats and a large round stone. My guess is that it was a milling stone that was decommissioned and is now displayed as an artifact. I wondered if the vats contained any kimchi fermenting away.

Maisan (Horse Ear Mountain)

Maisan is named becaused of the two peaks of the mountain that looks like a set of horse ears from afar. We trekked up the rocky path to the reach the Tapsa temple. On the way, we saw ajummas hiking up the mountain for exercise. Visitors are here to see the curious-looking stone pillars. They are formed entirely by stacking big rocks on top of each other and then filling the cracks with small stones.

Strawberry Farm

The visit to the strawberry farm was my favourite part of the trip. The strawberries were grown using the plasticulture method, further protected under an overhead shelter. This double protection eliminates the need for pesticides and the strawberries can mature in its best form. On this farm, we were free to eat the ripe fruits as we walk down the aisles. The cool air, golden sun ray, bright red fruits stuffed in mouth and sugar rush to the head was a potent mixture for happiness. We were smiling and squealing with joy as we eat our way through strawberry heaven.
The lifecycle of a strawberry start out as a white flower with a yellow center. After fertlisation, the white petals drop off and the center becomes a small furry green node. As it grows, it becomes white and shiny. It turns red when it is ripe.

Ice Gallery

In the Ice Gallery, miniature buildings and sculptures were carved out of ice blocks. The room temperature was kept below zero to keep all the ice frozen. We borrowed thick down jackets to keep warm. There was an ice slide that was not slippery and rooms with chairs and table all made of ice. The ice were mostly clear with no air bubbles allowing colored light to pass through. At the end of it, we got to carve an ice block to make an ice mug. I wasn't successful in keeping the handle attached to the mug. Ellis was sucessful and proudly showed off her work.

Ewha Womans University

While walking around, we chanced upon a beautiful university. The Ewha Womans University had wide paths with neatly trimmed shrubs and flowers. There was a mix of old brick buildings and modern steel and glass buildings. One building was four storeys underground but exposed one side to a long pavement. This bold design lets in natural light.

Till the next time

Shopping was part of the fun of travelling too. Looking at the items laid out, it is hard to believe we bought so many.
